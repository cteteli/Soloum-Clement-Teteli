MyTIPS – Mycorrhizal Tropical Inoculum Production System (Benin)

Grant Number MyTIPS/UP/2020-01
Period January 2020 – October 2020
Funder University of Parakou (UP) – MyTIPS Research Unit
Location Parakou, Benin
Role Research Intern / Field Agent
Partners Lab. d'Écologie, Botanique et Biologie Végétale – UP

Project Objective

To collect, identify, culture, and preserve mycorrhizal fungi (both ectomycorrhizal and arbuscular) from tropical soils in Benin; to build a comprehensive and functional fungal herbarium collection at the University of Parakou; and to produce standardized inoculum for field trials in agricultural and forestry contexts, thereby contributing to sustainable soil health management and the development of bio-inoculants adapted to West African conditions.

Soil Sampling Herbarium Mycorrhiza Benin Bio-inoculants
Activities Carried Out
01

Soil Sampling and Spore Isolation in Agricultural Fields

Jan – Mar 2020

Systematic collection of rhizosphere soil samples from farmers' fields across three agro-ecological zones of northern Benin (Sudan-Guinean, Sudanese, and sub-Sudanese zones). Application of wet sieving and sucrose centrifugation protocols for spore extraction. Morphological identification of arbuscular mycorrhizal fungi (AMF) spore morphotypes using reference keys.

02

Fungal Culture and Trap Cultures

Apr – Jun 2020

Establishment of trap cultures using sterilized substrate (quartz sand + expanded clay) inoculated with field soil samples, planted with host plants (Sorghum bicolor and Allium porrum). Maintenance of cultures in controlled greenhouse conditions for 4 months. Monitoring of spore production and root colonization every 2 weeks.

03

Herbarium Collection Development

Jul – Aug 2020

Collection, drying, mounting, and cataloguing of fungal specimens for the MyTIPS herbarium at the University of Parakou. Each specimen was labeled with collection site, date, host plant, and preliminary identification. A total of 85 AMF morphotype specimens and 32 macrofungal specimens were added to the institutional collection, enriching the reference base for future studies.

04

Field Data Collection and Farmer Interviews

Sept – Oct 2020

Structured interviews with 60 farmers to document traditional soil management practices, fertilizer use, crop rotation systems, and perceptions about soil biodiversity. GPS-referenced sampling sites were mapped and integrated into the project database. Collection of agronomic data (yield, soil organic matter) used to correlate mycorrhizal abundance with farming practices.
