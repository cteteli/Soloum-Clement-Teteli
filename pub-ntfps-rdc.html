Exploitation of non-timber forest products (NTFPs) in the eastern part of DR Congo

Authors: Mugisho S.M., Muhindo D.I., Balezi A., Ndeko J.B., Imani G.M., Teteli C.S. & Landry N.C. Year: 2022 Journal: Preprints Reference: 2022120575

Abstract

Non-timber forest products (NTFPs) play a critical role in the livelihoods, food security, and cultural practices of rural communities in the Congo Basin. This study examined the diversity, socio-economic importance, and sustainability of NTFP exploitation in the eastern part of DR Congo.

A comprehensive inventory was conducted in 8 villages across 3 territories of eastern DRC via interviews with 240 households. Results revealed 78 NTFP species regularly exploited, including edible fungi (Cantharellus spp., Termitomyces spp.) of high economic importance.

  • Keywords: NTFPs, Congo Basin, forest products, biodiversity, livelihoods, sustainable management, edible fungi
  • Study area: Eastern DR Congo (3 territories, 8 villages)
  • Methods: Participatory inventory, semi-structured interviews, focus groups, NTFP classification
  • Sample: 240 households, 8 villages across 3 territories
  • Main findings: 78 NTFP species identified; edible fungi (Cantharellus, Termitomyces) of high economic importance; unsustainable harvesting patterns documented
