Temperate Forests of Belgium – Mycoblitz Workshop: identification, eDNA & phylogenetics

Period: October 2024 Location: Ghent, Belgium Type: International scientific workshop Organiser: Ghent University (UGent) View on map

This scientific field trip took place within the "Mycoblitz – a lab in a backpack" programme organised by Ghent University. It immersed me in the world of European temperate forest mycology, with total immersion in modern field identification techniques, environmental DNA (eDNA) extraction and phylogenetic analysis, alongside researchers and doctoral students from 10+ countries.

Field Mission Objectives

Master the morphological identification of ectomycorrhizal fungi in temperate environments; collect soil samples for eDNA analyses; compare temperate fungal diversity (Belgium) to that of tropical zones (Benin); strengthen the international mycology research network.

  • Field excursion in 3 temperate forests of the Ghent region (beech forests, oak forests, maple forests)
  • Morphological field identification of 60+ species of ectomycorrhizal fungi
  • Collection of 45 rhizospheric soil samples for eDNA metabarcoding analysis
  • Microscopic observation of ECM morphotypes on fresh beech and oak roots
  • Laboratory eDNA extraction (DNeasy PowerSoil protocol) and quality control (Nanodrop, Qubit)
  • Comparative phylogenetic analysis with African sequences (MEGA11, MUSCLE)
  • Presentation of preliminary results at a closing seminar with international participants

Scientific Network & Collaborations

This field trip allowed the establishment of collaborations with researchers from Ghent University (Prof. Arne Verstraeten), the University of Liège (Belgium), INERA (DRC) and CIRAD (France), within a research network on Afro-European fungal diversity.
