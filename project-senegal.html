Mycorrhiza Culture and Fungal Inoculum Production for Tree Restoration in Senegal

Grant Number ISRA/LNRPV/2025-02
Period March 2025 – May 2026
Funder ISRA – Senegalese Institute of Agricultural Research
Location Dakar, Senegal (LNRPV – ISRA)
Role Researcher / Technical Trainer
Partners LNRPV-ISRA (Senegal) & Ghent University

Project Objective

To develop and optimize mycorrhizal inoculum production protocols adapted to native tree species of the Sahel, using locally available fungal strains. The project aims to strengthen the ecological restoration strategy of degraded ecosystems by providing low-cost, easily replicable biotechnological solutions for nurseries and reforestation programs in Senegal and the broader West Africa Sahel region.

Activities Carried Out
01

Baseline Assessment of Local Mycorrhizal Strains

Mar – Apr 2025

Inventory and preliminary characterization of native mycorrhizal fungal strains available at ISRA-LNRPV. Selection of promising strains based on their compatibility with target tree species (Acacia senegal, Parkia biglobosa, Vitellaria paradoxa). Molecular confirmation of strain identity using ITS sequencing.

02

Optimization of Inoculum Production Protocols

May – Aug 2025

Development and testing of three different inoculum production methods (spore-based, root fragment, and substrate-based inoculum). Comparison of production efficiency, shelf life, and infectivity on tree seedlings under nursery conditions. Statistical analysis of growth parameters (root colonization rate, plant height, biomass).

03

Training Workshop for Nursery Technicians

Nov 2025

Organization and facilitation of a 5-day practical training workshop for 20 nursery technicians and forest officers from the Ministry of Environment of Senegal. Topics covered: mycorrhizal biology, inoculum preparation, quality control, field application techniques, and monitoring of inoculation success in reforestation plots.

04

Field Validation of Inoculation Protocols

Jan – May 2026

Field-scale validation of the optimized inoculation protocols in 3 reforestation sites across different soil types in Senegal. Assessment of plant survival rates, growth improvement, and mycorrhizal colonization success over a 6-month monitoring period. Preparation of technical report and recommendations for national scaling.
