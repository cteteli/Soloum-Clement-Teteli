Integrated management of tropical lands and forests

Period: 2021 – 2023 Location: ERAIFT, DRC Type: Master's thesis (AGIFT) Institution: ERAIFT – UNESCO, Kinshasa

This master's thesis (Master in Integrated Management of Tropical Forests and Lands – AGIFT) focused on the study of agroforestry and soil conservation practices in the Sudanian zone of West Africa, with a focus on Benin. It evaluated the biophysical, socio-economic and environmental effectiveness of priority practices for erosion control, restoration of degraded lands and long-term maintenance of ecosystem services.

Research Questions

Which agroforestry practices are most effective for soil conservation in the Sudanian zone of Benin? How should these practices be prioritised taking into account biophysical criteria, their adoption by farmers and their socio-economic impact?

  • Comprehensive literature review on soil conservation practices in the Sudanian zone
  • Participatory surveys with 180 farmers across 3 municipalities in northern Benin
  • Multi-criteria analysis (AHP – Analytic Hierarchy Process) for practice prioritisation
  • Erosion risk zone mapping with GIS (ArcGIS, QGIS)
  • Evaluation of the impact of live hedges, stone bunds, zaï pits and grass strips
  • Analysis of ecosystem services associated with agroforestry practices
  • Publication of 2 scientific articles in peer-reviewed journals (Tropicultura, Alternatives Rurales)
