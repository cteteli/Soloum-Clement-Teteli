Study of mycorrhization in tropical agroecosystems

Period: 2024 – Ongoing Location: Parakou, Benin & Ghent, Belgium Institution: UGent & UP Type: PhD

This PhD thesis involves an in-depth analysis of the interactions between mycorrhizal fungi and crop plants in different tropical agroecosystems of Benin (West Africa). It aims to evaluate the impact of mycorrhization on soil health, agricultural productivity and the resilience of farming systems to environmental stresses, combining cutting-edge morphological and molecular approaches.

Main Objectives

Characterize the diversity of ectomycorrhizal (ECM) and arbuscular (AMF) fungi in tropical agroecosystems; evaluate their ecological functions; develop sustainable inoculation strategies for forest restoration and improvement of agricultural soil health.

  • Collection and morphological identification of mycorrhizal fungi from 15 study sites in northern Benin
  • Environmental DNA (eDNA) extraction and metabarcoding analysis (ITS2) by next-generation sequencing (NGS)
  • Phylogenetic and bioinformatic analysis with QIIME2, R/Phyloseq, UNITE database
  • Evaluation of mycorrhizal colonization rate of cultivated plant roots
  • Modelling of fungal species distribution according to pedoclimatic parameters
  • Development of inoculation protocols adapted to tropical conditions

Methods & Tools Used

Wet sieving, sucrose centrifugation, optical and electron microscopy, CTAB DNA extraction, PCR (ITS1F/ITS4 primers), Illumina MiSeq, R software, QIIME2, Phyloseq, MEGA, ArcGIS.
