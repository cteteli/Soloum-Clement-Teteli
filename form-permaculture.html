Permaculture and Regenerative Agriculture Training for Tropical Contexts

Period: March 2024 Location: Benin / Online Type: Practical training in sustainable agriculture

Course Description

This permaculture and regenerative agriculture training explores the principles and methods of agriculture inspired by natural ecosystems, aiming to create resilient, sustainable food production systems with high ecological value. For a researcher specialising in mycorrhizal fungi and soil-plant interactions, this training offers a practical and holistic perspective on how knowledge in microbial ecology can be integrated into alternative and regenerative agricultural systems.

  • Fundamental principles of permaculture: observation, connection, biomimicry, ethics (earth care, people care, fair share)
  • Garden and food system design in permaculture: zoning, sectors, plant guilds
  • Regenerative agriculture: soil fertility restoration practices (composting, mulching, no-till)
  • Role of mycorrhizal fungi in permaculture systems and regenerative agriculture
  • Agroforestry and permacultural design of tropical polyculture systems
  • Sustainable water management in tropical contexts (rainwater harvesting, swales, berms)
  • Application of permaculture principles in African contexts: adaptation to local constraints

Skills Acquired

Mastery of permaculture and regenerative agriculture principles and methods; design of resilient agroecological systems; in-depth understanding of the role of mycorrhizal fungi in sustainable food systems; connection between fundamental mycology research and practical applications in tropical permaculture.
