SPIBES: Biodiversity, Ecosystem Services & Climate Change – CABES, Côte d'Ivoire

Period: March 2024 Location: CABES, Côte d'Ivoire Type: Specialised biodiversity training

Course Description

The SPIBES (Sciences and Practices of Biodiversity and Ecosystem Services) training offered by CABES in Côte d'Ivoire provides comprehensive training on biodiversity assessment, ecosystem services valuation and adaptation to climate change in sub-Saharan African contexts. This programme deepened my understanding of the links between biodiversity conservation (including fungal biodiversity) and ecosystem service delivery.

  • Biodiversity assessment methods: indicators, rapid ecological surveys, biotic inventories
  • Ecosystem services frameworks (IPBES, TEEB): provisioning, regulating, cultural services
  • Climate change and biodiversity: observed and projected impacts on tropical African ecosystems
  • Conservation biology: protected areas, landscape corridors, nature-based solutions
  • Biodiversity and development: integration of biodiversity in environmental policies and decision-making
  • Soil biodiversity and its role in ecosystem functioning (focus on mycorrhizal fungi)
  • Participatory approaches for biodiversity monitoring with local communities

Skills Acquired

Biodiversity assessment and monitoring methods; ecosystem services valuation; understanding of IPBES/TEEB frameworks; conservation biology applied to African tropical contexts; climate change adaptation; links between soil biodiversity (mycorrhizal fungi) and ecosystem services.
