Ecosystem Services Assessment and Waste Management Awareness in Tshumba-kituti, DR Congo

Grant Number EU-ECO/RDC/2022-04
Period January 2022 – December 2022
Funder European Union (EU Development Fund)
Location Kinshasa & Tshumba-kituti, DR Congo
Role Field Facilitator / Environmental Educator
Partners AUF Club "Défenseurs de l'Environnement" & EU Delegation DRC

Project Objective

To inventory and assess ecosystem services in peri-urban areas of Kinshasa and the rural community of Tshumba-kituti (DR Congo); to raise community awareness on sustainable waste management and recycling practices; and to integrate ecosystem services valuation into local development planning frameworks through participatory approaches and capacity-building activities.

Waste Management Ecosystem Services Community Engagement Urban Ecology Capacity Building
Activities Carried Out
01

Ecosystem Services Inventory and Mapping

Jan – Mar 2022

Participatory inventory of provisioning, regulating, cultural, and supporting ecosystem services in two study areas (Kinshasa peri-urban zone and Tshumba-kituti village). Data collection through structured interviews with 150 households, GIS mapping of key ecosystem service areas, and assessment of service degradation levels due to urbanization and unsustainable land use.

02

Waste Management Awareness Campaigns

Apr – Jul 2022

Design and delivery of 8 community awareness sessions on sustainable waste management, involving over 400 residents across 5 neighborhoods. Topics included: waste sorting and classification, the 3R principle (Reduce, Reuse, Recycle), composting techniques, and the environmental and health consequences of open burning and illegal dumping.

03

Recycling Workshops and Practical Training

Aug – Oct 2022

Organization of 4 hands-on recycling workshops targeting youth, women's groups, and community leaders. Activities included upcycling of plastic bottles into garden pots, composting of organic waste, and creation of recycled-material handicrafts for income generation. Distribution of composting kits to 50 participating households.

04

Final Report and Policy Recommendations

Nov – Dec 2022

Compilation and analysis of all collected data. Preparation of a comprehensive technical report on ecosystem services valuation and waste management practices in both study sites. Development of concrete policy recommendations for the integration of ecosystem services into local development plans, submitted to the local municipal authorities and the EU delegation in DRC.
