Summer Field Course – Mycoblitz: Intensive Mycological Survey in Belgian Temperate Forests

Period: July 2024 Location: Temperate forests, Belgium Organiser: Ghent University (UGent) Type: Intensive field training (summer course)

Course Description

The Mycoblitz is an intensive summer field course organised as part of my doctoral training at Ghent University. This rapid and exhaustive mycological survey programme in Belgian temperate forests trains participants in macroscopic and molecular fungi identification techniques, eDNA sample collection, and bioinformatic methods of fungal phylogenomics. This course gave me direct experience with temperate forest fungi, very different from the African tropical species I usually work with.

  • Intensive mycological survey (Mycoblitz) in Belgian temperate forests: identification and collection of macro- and microscopic fungi
  • Morphological identification techniques for macromycetes (basidiomycetes and ascomycetes) in field conditions
  • Collection of environmental samples (eDNA: soil, litter, dead wood) for subsequent molecular analyses
  • Introduction to bioinformatics tools for fungal meta-barcoding (ITS1/ITS2) data analysis
  • Fungal phylogenomics: construction of phylogenetic trees and interpretation of evolutionary relationships
  • Participatory and citizen science: photographic documentation and contribution to mycological databases
  • Interdisciplinary scientific exchanges with European mycologists and field biologists

Skills Acquired

Morphological identification of fungi in European temperate forests; eDNA sample collection and packaging; practical introduction to mycological bioinformatics (QIIME2, ITS-barcoding); fungal phylogenomics and phylogenetic tree interpretation; participatory science and naturalist documentation.
