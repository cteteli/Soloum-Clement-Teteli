Prioritization of soil conservation antierosion practices in the common Sudanian zone in Benin (West Africa)

Teteli S.C., Padonou E.A. & Akakpo B.A. (2022)

Tropicultura, Vol. 40, Num. 3/4

This study analyses and ranks soil conservation practices to combat erosion in the Sudanian zone of Benin. It highlights priority practices based on their effectiveness, feasibility and acceptance by farmers, with concrete recommendations for sustainable land management.

2022

Exploitation of non-timber forest products (NTFPs) in the eastern part of DR Congo

Serge M. Mugisho, Daniel I. Muhindo, Alphonse Balezi, Julien B. Ndeko, Gerard M. Imani, Clement S. Teteli & Landry N.C. (2022)

Preprints 2022, 2022120575

This work addresses the diversity, socio-economic importance and sustainable management of non-timber forest products in eastern DRC. The results show the key role of these resources for local livelihoods and propose strategies for their valorisation and conservation.

2023

Priority agroforestry practices for soil conservation in the Sudanian zone of Benin

Teteli C.S., Padonou E.A., Langa A.M., Comlan G., Akabassi, Mukotanyi S.M. & Kokou B.K. (2023)

Alternatives Rurales, Num. 9 : 153–165

The article identifies and evaluates priority agroforestry practices for soil conservation in the Sudanian zone. The analysis takes into account their biophysical effectiveness, adoption by local populations and environmental impact.

Modelling the Current and Future Spatial Distribution Area of Adansonia digitata L. in the Context of Climate Change in Malawi (Southern Africa)

Kokou B.K., Salumu P.K., Balde IN, Lufungula J, Alunga G, Teteli S.C., et al. (2024)

Journal of Agricultural Science, 16(9)

This publication models the current and future spatial distribution of the baobab (Adansonia digitata) in Malawi under climate change scenarios. It provides distribution maps and recommendations for species conservation and management.
