Modelling the Current and Future Spatial Distribution Area of Adansonia digitata L. in the Context of Climate Change in Malawi (Southern Africa)

Authors: Kokou B.K., Salumu P.K., Balde IN, Lufungula J, Alunga G, Teteli S.C., et al. Year: 2024 Journal: Journal of Agricultural Science Volume: 16(9)

Abstract

Adansonia digitata L. (African baobab) is a keystone tree species of paramount ecological, cultural, and nutritional significance across sub-Saharan Africa. This study modelled the current and future (2050, 2070) spatial distribution of A. digitata in Malawi using MaxEnt species distribution modelling.

Results showed that temperature seasonality, mean annual precipitation, and soil type were the most influential factors. By 2070, the SSP5-8.5 scenario projects a 38% reduction in climatically suitable areas. The study identifies priority conservation areas and offers recommendations for national biodiversity strategies.

  • Keywords: Adansonia digitata, baobab, MaxEnt, species distribution modelling, climate change, Malawi, Southern Africa, CMIP6
  • Study area: Malawi, Southern Africa
  • Methods: MaxEnt SDM, CMIP6 climate scenarios (SSP2-4.5, SSP5-8.5), VIF analysis, ArcGIS mapping
  • Main findings: 38% reduction in suitable habitat predicted by 2070 under SSP5-8.5; temperature seasonality identified as key driver
  • Data: 387 occurrence records, WorldClim bioclimatic variables, GBIF, herbarium records
