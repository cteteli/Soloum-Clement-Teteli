Lama Forest, Benin – Mycological Field Mission

Period: June 2023 Location: Lama Forest, Benin Type: Doctoral field mission View on map

The Lama Forest is one of the most important semi-deciduous dense forests in Benin, classified as a biosphere reserve. This field mission constituted a crucial part of my thesis work, dedicated to the inventory of mycorrhizal fungal biodiversity and the understanding of fungi-plant interactions in this rare and threatened West African forest ecosystem.

Mission Objectives

Inventory the diversity of ectomycorrhizal fungi associated with dominant trees; collect soil and root samples for molecular analyses; document fungi-plant interactions through direct observation; train students from the University of Parakou in field mycology techniques.

  • Delimitation and systematic survey of 6 plots of 20×20 m in the dense forest
  • Collection of 180 rhizospheric soil samples under dominant species (Diospyros mespiliformis, Khaya senegalensis)
  • Harvest and identification of 85 macrofungi specimens (fruit bodies) with photographic documentation
  • Collection of mycorrhized roots for morphotypic and molecular analysis
  • Measurement of environmental parameters: soil pH, humidity, texture, organic matter
  • Field training of 8 students from the University of Parakou (L3 and Master's)
  • Conservation of specimens for the University of Parakou herbarium

Notable Observations

Discovery of several ECM morphotypes not yet described for the Lama Forest, particularly associations between Scleroderma spp. and Isoberlinia doka. Exceptional species richness with more than 40 morphotypes identified across the 6 plots, including several edible species valued locally.
