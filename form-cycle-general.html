General Agronomy Cycle – National University of Agriculture (UNA), Benin

Period: 2016 – 2018 Institution: National University of Agriculture (UNA) Location: Porto-Novo / Benin Level: Preparatory cycle (L1–L2)

Programme Overview

The General Agronomy Cycle at UNA corresponds to the first two years of the Bachelor's programme (L1 and L2), constituting the common core of agronomic training before specialisation. These two years provided fundamental foundations in natural sciences, chemistry, applied mathematics and biology, while introducing basic agronomic disciplines: pedology, botany, animal science and rural economics.

  • Mathematics and statistics applied to agronomic sciences
  • General chemistry, organic chemistry and soil/plant biochemistry
  • Cell biology and fundamental genetics
  • Botany: morphology, systematics and plant physiology
  • Pedology and soil science: formation, classification, physico-chemical properties
  • General animal science: animal nutrition, cattle production and tropical poultry farming
  • Introduction to African rural economics and sociology

Skills Acquired

Solid foundations in biology, chemistry and applied mathematics; introduction to fundamental agronomic disciplines (botany, pedology, animal science); development of scientific rigour and analytical reasoning skills; preparation for Bachelor's-level agronomic specialisation.
