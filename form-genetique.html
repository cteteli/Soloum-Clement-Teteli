Training in Molecular Genetics and Bioinformatics Applied to Mycology

Period: June 2024 Location: Belgium / Online Type: Specialised genetics & bioinformatics training

Course Description

This specialised training in molecular genetics and bioinformatics, followed as part of my doctoral programme at Ghent University, provided the technical skills necessary to analyse high-throughput sequencing data (meta-barcoding, amplicon sequencing) applied to the study of fungal diversity. The training covers standard bioinformatics tools for microbial community analysis: from raw sequencing data management through to ecological interpretation of results.

  • Molecular genetics: principles of next-generation sequencing (NGS), amplicons, fungal ITS-barcoding
  • Bioinformatics: processing and quality control of raw sequencing data (FastQC, Trimmomatic)
  • QIIME2 pipeline: read assembly, dereplication, OTU/ASV clustering, taxonomic assignment
  • Microbial diversity analysis: alpha diversity (Shannon, Chao1) and beta diversity (Bray-Curtis, UniFrac)
  • Phylogenomics: sequence alignment, phylogenetic tree construction (MUSCLE, IQ-TREE)
  • Multivariate statistical analyses in R: ordinations (PCoA, NMDS), permanova, visualisation with ggplot2
  • Practical application to eDNA sequencing data from mycorrhizal fungi in tropical agroecosystems

Skills Acquired

Mastery of the QIIME2 pipeline for fungal meta-barcoding analysis; bioinformatics skills (Linux, R, ggplot2, phyloseq); molecular genetics applied to microbial ecology; construction and interpretation of phylogenetic trees; multivariate statistical analyses of fungal communities.
