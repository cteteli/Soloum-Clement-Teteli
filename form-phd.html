PhD in Biology – Mycology & Ecology of Mycorrhizal Fungi

Period: 2024 – ongoing Ghent University (UGent) – Belgium University of Parakou (UP) – Benin Degree: PhD

Thesis Subject

My doctorate focuses on the ecology of arbuscular mycorrhizal fungi (AMF) in tropical agroecosystems of sub-Saharan Africa. The thesis aims to characterize the functional and molecular diversity of AMF in different cropping systems (cocoa, food crops, agroforestry systems), to evaluate their role in the resilience of agroecosystems facing anthropogenic disturbances, and to propose adapted inoculation strategies to sustainably improve agricultural yields under tropical conditions.

  • Morphological and molecular characterization (DNA meta-barcoding) of arbuscular mycorrhizal fungal (AMF) communities in tropical agroecosystems
  • Comparison of AMF diversity and functionality in different land-use systems (annual crops, cocoa, forests, fallows)
  • Evaluation of the effect of agricultural practices (fertilization, tillage, monoculture/polyculture) on AMF communities
  • Selection of AMF strains with high plant growth promotion (PGP) potential for inoculum production
  • Inoculation trials under controlled and semi-controlled conditions to validate effectiveness of selected inoculums
  • Training in bioinformatics tools (QIIME2, R, phyloseq) for meta-barcoding data analysis
  • International scientific collaboration within a UGent (Belgium) – University of Parakou (Benin) co-tutelle framework

Supervision

Thesis conducted under the supervision of promoters at Ghent University (Department of Biology, Mycology Research Group) and the University of Parakou (Ecology, Botany and Plant Biology Laboratory – LEBBV). The doctorate benefits from joint funding and regular scientific exchanges between both institutions.
