Using eDNA and Phylogenetics for the Study of Fungal Biodiversity

Period: July 2024 Location: Ghent, Belgium Type: International workshop Organiser: Ghent University

Participation in an advanced-level international workshop on the use of environmental DNA (eDNA) techniques and phylogenetic analyses for the characterisation of fungal biodiversity. Organised within the "Mycoblitz – a lab in a backpack" programme at Ghent University (Belgium), this workshop brought together researchers and doctoral students from more than 10 countries for an intensive week of field and laboratory work in Belgian temperate forests.

Workshop Objectives

Master eDNA extraction techniques from forest soil samples; perform metabarcoding analyses to identify fungal diversity; construct phylogenetic trees from ITS sequences; and interpret results with modern bioinformatics tools.

  • eDNA extraction from Belgian temperate forest soil samples using the PowerSoil protocol
  • PCR amplification of ITS1 and ITS2 regions (universal fungal markers)
  • Library preparation for Illumina MiSeq sequencing (2×250 bp)
  • Bioinformatic processing with QIIME2 (DADA2 denoising, UNITE taxonomy)
  • Alpha and beta diversity analysis with R/Phyloseq and vegan
  • Phylogenetic tree construction (Maximum Likelihood, MEGA11)
  • Mapping of fungal OTU distribution across studied forest sites

Bioinformatics Tools Mastered

QIIME2, DADA2, UNITE (ITS database), R (Phyloseq, vegan, ggplot2), MEGA11, iTOL (Interactive Tree of Life), FastQC, Trimmomatic.
