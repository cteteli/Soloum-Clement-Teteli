Professional Internship – MyTIPS Unit, Ecology, Botany & Plant Biology Laboratory, University of Parakou

Period: January – December 2020 Location: University of Parakou, Benin Laboratory: LEBBV Type: Long-duration professional internship

Internship Description

This one-year professional internship in the MyTIPS Unit of the Ecology, Botany and Plant Biology Laboratory (LEBBV) at the University of Parakou forms the foundation of my practical training in mycology and the ecology of mycorrhizal fungi. During this internship, I participated in all activities of the laboratory workflow: from field collection to culture under controlled conditions, morphological observation and management of the fungal collection (herbarium). This internship was decisive for my scientific training and directly led to my participation in the MyTIPS 2020 project collection campaigns.

  • Collection of arbuscular (AMF) and ectomycorrhizal (ECM) fungi from various study sites around Parakou
  • Morphological observation and identification of fungal species under binocular magnifier and optical microscope
  • Culture of mycorrhizal fungi under controlled conditions (culture room, specialised growth media)
  • Management and updating of the fungal collection in the Herbarium of the University of Parakou Ecology Laboratory
  • Participation in preparation of mycorrhizal inoculums used in MyTIPS project agronomic trials
  • Learning and application of wet sieving, sucrose centrifugation and acid fuchsin staining protocols
  • Contribution to data entry and management in the LEBBV fungal collection database

Skills Developed

Mastery of laboratory mycology techniques (culture, microscopic observation, morphological identification); management of a fungal collection and scientific herbarium; production of mycorrhizal inoculums under controlled conditions; application of standardised research protocols; scientific culture in plant biology and microbial ecology.
