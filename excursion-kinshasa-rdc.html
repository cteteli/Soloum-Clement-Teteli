Kinshasa & Tshumba-kituti, DRC – Environmental Mission and Ecosystem Services

Period: April 2022 Location: Kinshasa & Tshumba-kituti, DRC Type: EU / Master field mission Funding: European Union

This mission in the Democratic Republic of Congo took place within an EU-funded project, aiming to integrate the valorisation of ecosystem services into local development plans. It combined field inventory of ecosystem services in Kinshasa and in the rural community of Tshumba-kituti, with awareness activities on sustainable waste management.

Mission Objectives

Survey and evaluate ecosystem services in peri-urban and rural areas of DRC; raise community awareness on sustainable waste management and recycling; collect socio-environmental data to inform local development plans.

  • Participatory inventory of 4 categories of ecosystem services (provisioning, regulating, cultural, supporting) in 5 Kinshasa districts
  • Structured surveys with 150 households on their perceptions and uses of natural resources
  • Assessment of ecosystem degradation due to urbanisation and unsustainable practices
  • GPS mapping of key ecosystem areas (wetlands, green spaces, biological corridors)
  • Facilitation of 3 waste management awareness sessions with local communities
  • Practical training on waste sorting and composting in Tshumba-kituti
  • Data collection for ecosystem services mapping (ArcGIS support)

Lessons and Impacts

The mission documented significant degradation of ecosystem services in the peri-urban area of Kinshasa, linked primarily to poor waste management and uncontrolled urban sprawl. The recommendations produced were integrated into the final report submitted to the EU delegation in DRC.
