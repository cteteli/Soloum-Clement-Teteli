Bachelor's in Agronomic Sciences – National University of Agriculture (UNA), Benin

Period: 2016 – 2019 Institution: National University of Agriculture (UNA) Location: Porto-Novo / Benin Degree: Bachelor's in Agronomic Sciences

Programme Overview

The Bachelor's in Agronomic Sciences at the National University of Agriculture (UNA) in Benin is a three-year programme (LMD Bachelor's level) covering the fundamentals of tropical agronomy, soil science, crop and animal production, agro-economics and plant protection. This curriculum provided me with a solid scientific and technical foundation on which I build my research career in mycorrhizal fungi biology and ecology.

  • Soil science: pedology, soil fertility, organic matter and nutrient management
  • Tropical crop production: food crops, industrial crops, irrigation systems
  • Plant protection: phytopathology, agricultural entomology, integrated pest management methods
  • Agro-economics and rural economics: farm management, rural markets, extension services
  • Plant biology and botany: anatomy, physiology, systematics of cultivated and wild plants
  • Practical internships in agricultural farms and Beninese agronomic research centres
  • Bachelor's thesis on soil-plant interactions in tropical agroecosystems

Skills Acquired

Solid foundations in tropical agronomy, soil science and plant biology; practical skills in agronomic experimentation and cultivation techniques; mastery of soil analysis and phytosanitary diagnosis methods; introduction to agronomic research and scientific writing.
