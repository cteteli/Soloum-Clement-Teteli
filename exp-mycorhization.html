Mycorrhization Characterization Assistance in Cocoa Plantations and Local Species

Period: September – November 2023 Location: Benin Type: Scientific assistance mission

Mission Description

This mission aimed to characterize morphologically and molecularly the mycorrhizal associations in cocoa (Theobroma cacao) plantations and several local forest species of Benin, in order to identify the most effective mycorrhizal fungi for crop growth and stress resistance.

  • Collection of rhizospheric soil and root samples under cocoa plantations and local forests
  • Isolation of mycorrhizal fungal spores by wet sieving and sucrose centrifugation
  • Morphological observation and identification of spores under binocular magnifier and optical microscope
  • Observation of mycorrhized roots after trypan blue staining (Phillips & Hayman method)
  • Calculation of mycorrhizal colonization rate by the grid intersection method
  • Identification of soil fungi associated with cocoa roots
  • Writing a technical report on observed mycorrhizal associations

Skills Developed

Mastery of sieving and centrifugation techniques for AMF spore extraction; microscopic observation and morphotypic identification; calculation and interpretation of mycorrhizal colonization rates; technical scientific report writing.
