Research Activities

Study of mycorrhization in tropical agroecosystems

PhD thesis
2024 – Ongoing Parakou, Benin & Ghent, Belgium

In-depth analysis of the interaction between mycorrhizal fungi and crop plants in various tropical agroecosystems. Evaluation of the impact of mycorrhization on soil health, productivity and resilience of agricultural systems. Use of morphological and molecular techniques (eDNA, DNA barcoding).

Conservation and valorisation of forest fungi in Africa

Collaborative project
2023 – 2024 Lama Forest, Benin

Project to survey, identify and valorise indigenous forest fungal species in Benin. Training workshops for local communities, development of science outreach materials, and phylogenetic analyses for the conservation of fungal genetic resources.

Using eDNA and phylogenetics for fungal biodiversity

Training / Workshop
July 2024 Ghent, Belgium

Participation in an international workshop on eDNA extraction, phylogenetic analysis and mapping of fungal diversity using advanced next-generation sequencing techniques. Field work in European forest environments with researchers from multiple nationalities.

Integrated management of tropical lands and forests

Master's thesis
2021 – 2023 ERAIFT, DRC

Study of agroforestry and soil conservation practices in the Sudanian zone of West Africa. Assessment of their effectiveness for erosion control, restoration of degraded lands and long-term maintenance of ecosystem services.
