Training in Environmental Law and Protection – IFDD (OIF) & Université Senghor

Period: May 2022 Institution: IFDD (OIF) & Université Senghor Type: Online specialised training

Course Description

This training in environmental law and protection, offered by the Institut de la Francophonie pour le Développement Durable (IFDD, OIF) in partnership with Université Senghor, covers the main international and national environmental law instruments relevant to biodiversity conservation, sustainable development and environmental governance in developing countries. This training gives me a critical legal perspective complementary to my scientific research.

  • International environmental law: major multilateral environmental agreements (CBD, CITES, UNFCCC, Paris Agreement)
  • Legal protection of biodiversity: access and benefit-sharing (Nagoya Protocol), species protection
  • Environmental governance in developing countries: national environmental law, legal frameworks for forests
  • Environmental liability and criminal law: offences, sanctions, environmental justice
  • Environmental impact assessment: legal obligations, procedures and implementation
  • Indigenous peoples' rights and traditional knowledge in international environmental law
  • Legal instruments for sustainable forest and natural resource management in Africa

Skills Acquired

Understanding of international and national environmental law; knowledge of legal frameworks for biodiversity conservation (CBD, Nagoya Protocol); ability to situate scientific research in its legal and regulatory context; understanding of environmental governance in developing countries; critical skills for implementing research in compliance with environmental laws.
