Priority agroforestry practices for soil conservation in the Sudanian zone of Benin

Authors: Teteli C.S., Padonou E.A., Langa A.M., Comlan G., Akabassi, Mukotanyi S.M. & Kokou B.K. Year: 2023 Journal: Alternatives Rurales Pages: 153–165

Abstract

Agroforestry is increasingly recognized as a key strategy for sustainable land management in sub-Saharan Africa. This study identifies and evaluates priority agroforestry practices for soil conservation in the Sudanian zone of Benin.

The study was conducted in three municipalities of northern Benin with 160 farmers. Key findings indicate that parkland agroforestry (integration of Vitellaria paradoxa, Parkia biglobosa, Faidherbia albida), alley cropping, and live fences were ranked as the highest-priority practices.

  • Keywords: Agroforestry, soil conservation, Sudanian zone, Benin, land degradation, multi-criteria analysis, smallholder farmers
  • Study area: Sudanian zone, northern Benin
  • Methods: PRA, semi-structured interviews, multi-criteria scoring, field assessment
  • Key species: Vitellaria paradoxa, Parkia biglobosa, Faidherbia albida
  • Main findings: Parkland agroforestry and alley cropping ranked as top priority; live fences also highly effective
