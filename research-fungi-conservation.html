Conservation and valorisation of forest fungi in Africa

Period: 2023 – 2024 Location: Lama Forest, Benin Type: Collaborative project Institution: University of Parakou

This collaborative project aimed at the exhaustive survey, taxonomic identification and valorisation of indigenous forest fungal species in Benin, particularly in the Lama Forest ecosystem. It combined intensive field approaches, community training workshops and phylogenetic analyses to contribute to the sustainable conservation of West African fungal genetic resources.

Objectives

Survey and identify indigenous forest fungal species of Benin; develop science outreach tools for local communities; produce phylogenetic analyses for the conservation of fungal genetic resources; strengthen local capacity in tropical mycology.

  • Survey of more than 120 macrofungi species in the Lama Forest (Benin)
  • Morphological and molecular identification (ITS barcoding) of collected species
  • Creation of an illustrated catalogue of fungal species from the Lama Forest
  • Organisation of 3 mycology training workshops for forest rangers and local communities
  • Comparative phylogenetic analysis of Beninese species vs African databases
  • Deposition of reference specimens in the University of Parakou herbarium

Partners & Collaborators

University of Parakou (Benin), Ghent University (Belgium), agents of the Benin Directorate of Forests and Natural Resources (DFRN), riverside communities of the Lama Forest.
